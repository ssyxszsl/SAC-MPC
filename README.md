Decision-making and motion planning are fundamental components of autonomous vehicels, directly affecting safety, efficiency, and ride comfort in interactive traffic scearios. Reinforcement learning (RL) based methods demonstrate promising performance through data-driven policy learning but lack explicit safety guarantees, limiting their applicability in safety-critical scenarios. To address this limitation, this paper proposes a safety-aware integrated framework that combines learning-based decision-making with model-based trajectory optimization. Specifically, a Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) agent is adopted for high-level decision-making, while a model predictive control (MPC) module is employed at the low level to generate smooth and dynamically feasible trajectories. To further enhance safety during both training and deployment, a Risk Value Network is introduced and trained online to estimate short-horizon collision risk from state–action pairs. The predicted risk is incorporated into reward shaping to guide policy optimization and is also utilized as a safety supervisor during deployment, where it triggers a conservative fallback strategy when elevated risk is detected.
